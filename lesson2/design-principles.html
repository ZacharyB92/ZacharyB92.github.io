Design Principles

Visual Hierachy

Penguin

I love the analogy of the squeaky wheel gets the grease. Penguin uses visual hierarchy very well to make things stand out to their users. When you go onto their page you immeadiately know what they are trying to promote, their 40% discount. They also use color and contrast to get things to stand out.

White Space and Clean Design

Coca-Cola

Coca-Cola uses White Space and Clean Design very well. When you go onto their page you dont see a cluttered mess but rather you see great use of color and space. Things feel very organized like you just stepped into a clean well organized house, it makes you want to stay. The white space allows for the images and other colors on the page to tell their stoy better than if everyhting was Coca-Cola red.

Constrast

Deloitte.

One thing that I really enjoy about Deloitte's styling is the use of contrast with color. They have an awesome lime green color that stands out no matter what color you put it up against. They use it to identify with but also to make things stand out on their page. For example the "Featured" and "Trending" tabs are easier to see because of the use of the contrasting colors.
